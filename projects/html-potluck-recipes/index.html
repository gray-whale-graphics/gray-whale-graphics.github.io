Frozen Butterbeer

Prep Time: 5 min    Cook Time: 5 min    Servings: 6

Get the original recipe here

Ingredients

For each serving:

  • 6 cups Ice cubes (enough to fill a gallon-sized freezer bag about half way)
  • 1 Tbsp. Kosher salt (Ice cream salt or table salt will work too)
  • 1 cup cream soda
  • 1 tsp. butterscotch ice cream topping

For the Cream Topping:

  • 1 c. heavy cream or whipping cream
  • 1/4 c. butterscotch topping

Instructions

Pre-Prep: Place ice cubes and salt in a gallon-sized Ziploc bag.

For the Slush:

  1. Place cream soda and butterscotch in a smaller quart-sized Ziploc bag, press out all the air and seal tightly. Mix around until the butterscotch is mixed in with the cream soda.
  2. Place bag of soda IN the bag of ice and seal.
  3. Shake for about 3-5 minutes or until the soda is nice and slushy - if it gets too cold, wrap a dish cloth around it and continue shaking.
  4. Pour slush into a cup and top with butterscotch cream topping (below).

For the Cream Topping:

  1. Beat the cream and butterscotch with a mixer on high speed until stiff peaks form.
  2. Top the slush mix with the cream topping and enjoy!

Pumpkin Pasties

Prep Time: 40 min    Cook Time: 18 min    Servings: 10

Get the original recipe here

Ingredients

For the Pumpkin Pasties:

  • 2 pie crusts, store bought or homemade
  • 3 oz (85 g) cream cheese, softened
  • 1/3 cup (73 g) brown sugar
  • 2 tsp (5 g) cornstarch
  • 3/4 cup (183 g) canned pumpkin puree, at room
  • 1 egg yolk, at room temperature
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 2 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 tbsp (15 ml) heavy cream

For the Egg Wash & Sugar Topping:

  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1/4 cup (50 g) granulated white sugar
  • 1/2 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • turbinado sugar, for sprinkling

Instructions

Putting Together the Pumpkin Pasties:

  1. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper and set aside.
  2. Add the cream cheese, brown sugar and cornstarch to a large bowl. Mix with an electric mixer on high speed until smooth and creamy, about 2 minutes.
    3 oz (85 g) cream cheese, softened,1/3 cup (73 g) brown sugar,2 tsp (5 g) cornstarch
  3. Add in the pumpkin puree, egg yolk, vanilla, pumpkin pie spice and heavy cream. Mix on medium speed until smooth.
    3/4 cup (183 g) canned pumpkin puree, at room ,1 egg yolk, at room temperature,1 tsp vanilla,2 tsp pumpkin pie spice,1 tbsp (15 ml) heavy cream
  4. Roll out the 2 pie crusts on a lightly floured surface and cut 20 mini pumpkins out using a 4" pumpkin cookie cutter. You will need to re-roll each pie crust to get all 20. (You should be able to cut 10 out of each pie crust.)
    2 pie crusts, store bought or homemade
  5. Transfer 10 of the pumpkins to the prepared baking sheets. (5 per each.)
  6. Add 1 heaping tbsp of pumpkin pie filling on the center of the 10 pumpkins on the baking sheets. (There will be a little bit of filling leftover.)
  7. Use a pastry brush to brush the edges with egg wash and place another pumpkin over top of each. Press the edges shut with a fork and cut a small lightning bolt into the center of each. Chill in the fridge for 20 minutes.
    1 egg, beaten

Baking the Pumpkin Pasties:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees while the pumpkin pasties chill.
  2. Combine the granulated sugar and pumpkin pie spice in a small bowl.
    1/4 cup (50 g) granulated white sugar, 1/2 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  3. Remove one of the cookie sheets of pumpkin pasties from the fridge and brush the tops with egg wash. Then sprinkle each pumpkin with the spiced sugar and a little bit of turbinado sugar.
    turbinado sugar, for sprinkling,1 egg, beaten
  4. Bake for 17-19 minutes, or until lightly golden brown. Then let the pumpkin pasties cool on the cookie sheet for 5 minutes. Transfer to a wire rack to cool until cool enough to handle, about 15 more minutes.
  5. Repeat the steps with the remaining cookie sheet of pumpkin pasties.
