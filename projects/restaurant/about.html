Our Story

Our family came to the United States in the late 1800s as Volga German immigrants, fleeing the Russification policies and violent village raids that made life in Russia increasingly dangerous. Though they had lived in Russia for generations, their German identity and way of life were under threat. Seeking safety and freedom, they made the long journey to America, where they put down new roots. Here, they built a life from the ground up—farming, tending the land, and preserving the recipes and traditions that had sustained them for centuries.
